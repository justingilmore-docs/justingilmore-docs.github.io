# [LEGACY] Involved Parties and Signatory Parties Policy

**Overview**
For international shipments utilizing Marketplace Fulfillment Services, specific legal entities must be identified to ensure regulatory compliance with U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP). Failure to accurately designate these parties results in a disruption of the supply chain and potential seizure of inventory.

**Importer of Record (IoR)**
The Seller, or a third party designated by the Seller, must act as the Importer of Record (IoR). The IoR is the party responsible for:
1. Ensuring the imported goods comply with all local laws and regulations.
2. Filing the completed duty entry and associated documents.
3. Paying the assessed import duties and other taxes on those goods.

**Ultimate Consignee**
The Ultimate Consignee is the party to whom the overseas shipper delivers the merchandise. For all Fulfillment Services shipments, the Marketplace Fulfillment Center address must be listed as the delivery location; however, the Marketplace Entity itself NEVER acts as the Importer of Record or the Ultimate Consignee for legal or customs purposes.

**Signatory Authority**
The Seller must provide a Power of Attorney (PoA) to their chosen Customs Broker to allow for the legal filing of entry documents. The Marketplace Entity will not sign any customs documents or provide PoA for any Seller-owned inventory.
